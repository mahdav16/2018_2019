UNIVERSITY OF TORONTO
AEROSPACE TEAM - UTAT

Rocketry is one of the four technical divisions at the University of Toronto Aerospace Team. It was founded in 2012, with the goal of developing high-altitude, hybrid sounding rockets for the purpose of atmospheric and reduced-gravity research.

TU WIEN
SPACE TEAM - TUST

Founded in 2010, TUST is the major aerospace student club in Austria. Due to the lack of an institute or faculty for aerospace at TU Wien, the students took it upon themselves to close that gap. Today, TUST develops solid boosted rockets, cubesats, and propulsion technology.

THE BASE11 SPACE CHALLENGE

The Base11 Space Challenge is a student tocketry competition, with the goal of designing, building, and launching a liquid bi-propelled rocket to space. By participating in this challenge, TXV aims to break the Karman line before the end of 2021. With fierce competition from fellow Canadian and American student teams, TXV is harnessing the power of competition-driven innovation and its own team heritage to power the development of its Houbolt Launch Vehicle.

DEADLINE

The rocket must complete its mission before December 21,2021.

TARGET ALTITUDE

The rocket must reach an altitude of 100km, the edge of space.

ROCKET TYPE

The rocket must be single stage and liquid bi-propelled.

PRIZE

The winning team recieves a $1 Million USD monetary prize.
